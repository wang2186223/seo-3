Chapter 731

"Cedric, does Daniela even realize how mean you are?" Alexander shouted, voice rising like a storm.

"Maybe she doesn't. But your incompetence? That's one thing she knows all too well."

Alexander nearly choked on his anger. "You're just the richest man in the country. What gives you the right to be so damn smug?"

Cedric smirked, unbothered. "Smug? Of course I am.Before leaving, Daniela handed me a fortune worth tens of billions."

Cedric tilted his head, feigning confusion. "Wait,didn't you marry Daniela once? Just curious-what did she leave you when she divorced you?"

That question jabbed straight into Alexander's unhealed wound.

In Olisvine and throughout the business world, it was no secret-Daniela had handed Elite Lux to Cedric.

So naturally, peopole kept pestering Alexander-what had Daniela given him in the divorce?

Alexander clenched his jaw tight, fury sealing his lips shut.

Even now, he couldn't form a comeback. He could only scoff coldly. "Only a pathetic man expects something from a woman after a divorce."

Cedric let out a mocking laugh. "Really? Then where is the inheritance Doug left for Joyce?"

That particular shame had long slipped from Alexander's mind. No one had dared bring it up around him until Cedric casually tossed it out. Rage ignited instantly.

"Cedric!" Alexander snapped, his tone turning sharp."Daniela's gone. No matter what she handed you, the fact remains-she left you behind. She didn't stick around for either of us. One word from her and you're stuck here, just like me. You're no different-just another piece she discarded. She's done with you. Once Daniela makes up her mind, there's no going back. She won't return to you. I've begged her more times than I can count. I repented again and again.I even reshaped myself to look like you, but in the end,she still turned her back on me. Daniela plays the game with precision-calculated, untouchable. Now that she's aligned herself with someone stronger,she's cast you aside too."

Alexander hurled his words like daggers, sharp and merciless.

A twisted sense of relief washed over him.

He'd never imagined the day would come when Cedric would be caught in the same storm he once endured.

Laughter burst from him-wild, mocking,laced with smug satisfaction.

Later that night, Joyce sought Cedric out. "Will you help me?"

Cedric already understood what she was after. "This isn't about me-it's for Jack," she added."What Alexander has should've been mine. Apart from Daniela, you're the only one who can help."

Joyce had schemed for ages, yet still had empty hands. But the bitterness refused to fade.

"I know Fairburne Group has merged with Bennett Group now, and the financial details are all tangled.But back then, Doug owned assets worth billions.I can't stomach being pushed out like I never mattered. Cedric, I'm asking for your help-for Jack's sake, the one Daniela once held dear."

None of her earlier words carried the weight of her final plea.

"Alright," Cedric answered with calm restraint.

Joyce exhaled, a faint smile curling on her lips."Cedric, if I ever get the chance, I swear I'll repay you."

Cedric didn't flinch. As he climbed the stairs, he said flatly, "You owe me nothing. I'm not doing this for you." And in that moment, Joyce understood. It was all because of Daniela. Back then, Daniela had been willing to help Jack.

When Daniela left, she didn't just hand over her fortune to Cedric-she also made sure Jack had a school and money for his future. Beyond that, she left no other strings.

Maybe it was the echo of her own loss that made her care so deeply for Jack.

In that instant, Joyce realized Cedric probably wasn't doing this for her-or even for Jack.

Cedric just needed to hold on to whatever thread of Daniela remained.

That was all.

In Oiscoll, Daniela received a message.

She tapped it open.

A familiar voice played in her ears.

"I'm not waiting for her," it said.
