Chapter 737

Daniela stayed quiet for a long moment, her expression unreadable.

Hamilton narrowed his eyes. "No matter how things turn out, whether or not you get the answers you're chasing, as long as Cedric accepts me as his father,a part of the McCoy fortune will be his. It's a risk-free deal. Daniela, you should know which path to choose. And as Cedric's wife, don't you want to see him make his mark in the business world, instead of wasting his days in the kitchen like some housewife?"

Hamilton wasn't sure if it was just his imagination,but as he finished speaking, he thought he caught a flash of deadly rage burning in Daniela's eyes.

A cold shiver slid down Hamilton's spine. "What?" he asked, feeling uneasy. "Did I say something wrong?"

Daniela's voice turned sharp as ice. "Don't you think you did?"

Hamilton retorted, "You think it's a good thing for him to be trapped in a kitchen?"

Daniela responded calmly, "Whether it's good or bad depends on whether he's happy. What others think means nothing to me, and it shouldn't matter to him either."

Hamilton bristled at Daniela's firm stance. "So, you're telling me you won't persuade him to come back to the McCoy family?"

Before she met Hamilton, Daniela had been unsure.

She had never known a perfect family herself, so she wished Cedric could have one.

She had dreamed of Cedric having parents, siblings,and someone he loved at his side.

She had wished for him a life filed with success and comfort.

But after meeting the McCoy family, she knew her answer. "I won't try to convince him," she said,her voice firm.

Hamilton's gaze sharpened. "You're willing to walk away fromn all that money?"

"No one hates money," Daniela said evenly. "But every fortune comes with a price. Accepting the McCoy family's wealth would mean Cedric severing ties with everything he once was. It means throwing away his pride just to live under your thumb-watching your every mood, serving you like a servant while you dine. To me, that's not love at all-it's nothing but control. I won't allow my partner to bow to anyone for the sake of money. He has his own pride and self-worth.When he was just a child, with no one to lean on, he fought to pave his own way.Now that he has me, he doesn't need to bow to anyone for wealth. He's free to live as he pleases,and no one has the right to stand in his way."

Daniela spoke with a calmness that only sharpened the weight of her words.

Hamilton locked eyes with her impassive face. "You really think you can keep him safe?" His words thickened the air, pulling the room into a stifling silence.

Daniela raised her eyes, catching sight of the army of mercenaries stationed outside the villa.

Hamilton leisurely lifted his cup, his voice dripping with authority. "Daniela, this is not your homeland.This is Oiscoll-a country | carved out with my own two hands."

Hamilton fully expected Daniela's composure to crack after hearing that. He waited for fear to flicker in her eyes. He imagined she'd finally grasp the weight of his words and tread carefully.

But to his shock, Daniela stayed even calmer than him. She lifted her cup, took a slow sip, placed it down, and smiled-a cool, effortless confidence in her every move.

"You're certainly impressive. But would you like to find out if your mercenaries draw their blades faster -or if my trigger finger is quicker?"

No sooner had the words left her mouth than the icy barrel of a black gun touched Hamilton's temple.

The secretary standing nearby froze, his face draining of color. "Oh no, Ms. Harper! Please, don't be upset! Mr. McCoy was only joking! Your husband is his son-he wouldn't harm him! You're all family here. It was just a harmless joke! Please don't take it to heart!"

The secretary had turned ghostly pale, beads of sweat glistening on his forehead.

Daniela sat there, the picture of unshakable calm.

Hamilton, still seated opposite her, regarded Daniela with a cold, assessing stare.

Never before had anyone dared press a gun to his head.

Daniela saw right through his fury and smiled. "Well,this is no joke. And just so we're clear-I don't find jokes funny. Understand?"

She kept the gun steady and added coolly, "I don't care what Cedric chooses later. But from this moment on, if even a hair on his head is harmed, I'l come for you, McCoy Group, and every last person tied to your name."

The secretary trembled like a leaf. "Ms. Harper,why are you being so unreasonable?"

Daniela locked eyes with Hamilton, neither side willing to blink first.

If Daniela ever stood against him, she would be a force even he would fear.

But if she stood with his heir, she would become the sharpest weapon in his arsenal, capable of wiping out anything that stood in their way.

Among all the men in the McCoy family, not a single one could match Daniela's iron will and fearless spirit.

Hamilton slowly nudged the barrel away from his temple with two fingers before finally speaking. "No need to get so heated. He's my son; I would never harm him. Daniela, I see your stance clearly. Solet me ask you-what if Cedric chose to come back to the McCoy family on his own?"
