Chapter 182

The entire room went silent, the air thick with anticipation as everyone waited.

"I'm sorry, but I don't have a clear answer." Daniela's steady voice cut through the stillness. "I've never truly understood what it means to love someone. So,I can't say if I ever loved you."

"Then why did you treat me so well?" Alexander's eyes brimmed with pain, staring at her as if she were a coldhearted liar-a woman who had played with his feelings just to leave him broken.

Daniela let out a quiet sigh. There wasn't a trace of guilt or remorse in her expression, only a calm sense of finality.

"I'm not sure if what I felt for you was love, but the care I showed you was genuine, Back when we were kids, you treated me with kindness, so it was only natural for me to want to give that back. I also believed that marriage wasn't built on fleeting emotions like passion-it needed effort, dedication,and commitment. Passionate love fades fast. By marrying you, I thought I was proving my sincerity and my desire to create a life together. But it hasn't been that long since we divorced. Do I really need to remind you why it ended?"

Her tone remained steady and calm,each word chosen with care. "You had an affair with Joyce.You were the one who gave me an ultimatum-either kneel and apologize or walk away. Maybe I didn't know if I loved you, but I do know one thing: love means loyalty; love means taking someone's side. If I didn't love you, then you didn't love me either. And because of that, Alexander, you have no right to point fingers at me."

Daniela's logic was unshakable.

At that moment, the reality hit everyone in the room like a cold gust of wind.

There was no denying it-Daniela had never really been in love with Alexander. After all, true love-wild, messy, and consuming-never lets anyone escape unscathed. Sure, Daniela might have been hurt. But without love, the pain didn't stick; it didn't carve any lasting wounds.

Now, she could discuss it with absolute ease,treating it like it was a trivial annoyance rather than something meaningful.

Daniela wasn't certain if she ever loved Alexander.

Yet, anyone who had ever been in love could see that Daniela truly didn't love Alexander.

And this truth was even more painfully obvious to Alexander.

He couldn't put into words the ripping sensation inside him when Daniela spoke.

His gaze towards Daniela was icy.

"Daniela! I can't believe you! All this time, you've been making a fool out of me!"

He had never claimed to have feelings for Joyce. He was just fed up with Daniela's unshakable composure-the kind that made it seem like nothing,no matter how big, could ever faze her.

Everyone thought Daniela loved him, but Alexander had always sensed there was something off-something he couldn't quite put his finger on.

All these years, he had struggled to figure out what it was.

So,he'd tried to provoke her jealousy.

He thought being around Joyce might awaken the feelings Daniela had kept locked away.

Yet, even after their divorce, he was left with nothing.

Today, he finally realized the truth. The barrier between them wasn't something he could fix. It was her heart, unreachable and distant.

Even so, he wasn't willing to let go. He opened his mouth, prepared to voice the one question that had been consuming him: If he offered to start over, would she take him back?Love or no love, he was determined to make her fall for him this time!

This time, he'd do it right.

Cedric noticed the resolve and the storm of emotions building in Alexander's gaze.

But before Alexander could say a word, Cedric stepped in, his imposing figure blocking Alexander's view of Daniela completely. "Haven't you said enough?"

Alexander's head whipped towards Cedric, his jaw tightening.

"If you're done, then get out!" Cedric added.

Alexander glared at Cedric, who stood a full head taller. "This is between Daniela and me."

Cedric gave a small nod. "If you two were still married, I might have stayed out of this. But you're not. She's not your wife anymore, Alexander. You made your choice, and it was the wrong one." The harsh lighting above threw shadows over Alexander's pale, tense features.

Then Cedric dropped the final bomb, his voice cold and firm. "Once you make your move, there's no going back. You've lost."
