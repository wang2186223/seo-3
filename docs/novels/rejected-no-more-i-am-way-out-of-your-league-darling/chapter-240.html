Chapter 236

Cedric exhibited patience and tenderness.

Daniela shook her head gently. "That's all."

With a warm smile, Cedric reached for Daniela's hand.

He sensed her initial hesitation and did not grasp it immediately. Instead, he waited until she loosened her fingers slightly before taking her hand once more.

"Daniela," Cedric called out as the morning light illuminated his face, making his eyes sparkle with the reflection of her. "I'm not Caiden; I'm Cedric. You are not your mother; you are Daniela, a remarkable woman in your own right. Your strength doesn't intimidate me; it makes me want to ensure your happiness even more. Your power doesn't scare me;it fills me with pride. I'm not without ambitions; I have my pride and my own capabilities. So, Daniela, is it me you doubt, or yourself?"

Cedric was not one to speak often, but his words always carried weight and assurance.

Now, as he spoke, Daniela found herself unable to dispute his points. She clenched her hand, yet she hesitated to agree outright.

Cedric asked, "You're thinking it's all happening too quickly, aren't you? Change is always a bit daunting,but don't let the unfamiliarity deter you from moving forward,right?"

Daniela found no words to counter him.

"Feeling sleepy?" Cedric kept his gaze steady, his smile convincing. "If you're tired, why not rest here in the living room? If not, perhaps we could go register our marriage."

Daniela blinked in surprise. "Are you in such a rush?"

Her mind was indeed cluttered at the moment.

She had been buried in paperwork, had a draining discussion with Caiden, and now was navigating this moment with Cedric.

Her thoughts were tangled.

She felt a thorough discussion was necessary to clarify their relationship.

Cedric was naturally aware of Daniela's reservations.

He understood her concerns perfectly.

Determined, he admitted he would use any means necessary to secure Daniela's commitment today.

If he waited until tomorrow, once Daniela had time to think it over, he feared he might lose his chance.

Though anxious, Cedric hid his feelings well, asking smoothly, "Any more doubts? Let's discuss them."

He maintained a calm demeanor while subtly pressing forward.

Daniela responded with a simple, "No."

"Is it the pace that bothers you? Do you think we're moving too quickly?" Cedric asked further.

Daniela confirmed with a nod.

"Too quickly, you say?" Cedric asked. "I've made my choice, and you've given your promise. It's inevitable,whether it happens today or tomorrow. Does the timing truly make a difference?"

Daniela found her thoughts increasingly scrambled by Cedric's arguments.

Cedric continued, "If we are destined together, why not fully embrace our future now? You'll need to come to terms with this eventually, and I'm here to support you as you adjust."

Daniela bit her lip thoughtfully. "How much time will you give me to adapt?"

"That's for you to decide. Whenever you're ready,we'll share the same bed."

Daniela blushed deeply. "That's not what | was referring to." Cedric laughed. "Of course, not that. Let's secure our marriage certificate today. As for the rest, you set the pace. It's only fair. You once promised me a favor,and this is all l ask."

Daniela felt cornered.

It seemed Cedric had laid out a strategic plan,and she was inadvertently playing into his hands at every turn.
