Chapter 241

It was obvious Cedric had no interest in Shirley.

Katrina's enthusiasm quickly faded, while Shirley stared at the closed door, her eyes reflecting deep thoughts.

"Aunt, let me try again," said Shirley.

Upon hearing this, Katrina looked up at Shirley with surprise.

Shirley's eyes shone with a sparkle, and a shy expression settled on her face, making her look just like a young girl experiencing the sweetness of first love.

Katrina gave a firm nod. "Alright, stay at home. Let me tell you, a woman can easily capture a man's heart if she makes thefirst move. You must be proactive, understand?"

Shirley responded with a nod. "I understand. Don't worry, I feel confident."

Katrina's face lit up with joy.

Caiden observed silently, his patience tested once more.

Was she really teaching her niece how to seduce a man?

Katrina would stop at nothing for money.

Katrina caught the sneer in Caiden's gaze and scoffed, "When Brylee was alive, didn't you flirt with me as well? Men often let their desires make their decisions and none are any good!"

Even with his knowledge of Katrina's character, Caiden was shocked by her blunt words.

"You used to praise me, calling me elegant and composed, a true gentleman. How can you speak like this now? You told me your ex-husband abused you when he was drunk, and I felt sympathy for you. After Brylee's funeral, I married you, promising never to let you suffer. And now, you're telling me that I'm just like those awful men?"

Caiden felt a deep pain in his heart.

Unmoved, Katrina looked at Caiden with hatred and exclaimed, "Caiden, why pretend to be so affectionate?If you were truly devoted, you wouldn't have cheated with me. By that sense, you're worse than those other men."

The illusion was completely destroyed.

Caiden's face turned pale with rage, his body trembling. What he had believed to be love was nothing but a lie.

His fsts clenched tightly, he gave a bitter, trembling laugh.

"Fine! You really are something," Caiden said, his expression filled with anger. "Katrina, l've finally seen your true colors."@

Katrina appeared unaffected.

Nobody enjoyed serving others, especially not fora lifetime.

She understood that Caiden's declared love for her was merely a search for the dominance he could not find with Brylee.

If she pleased him, he was more than happy.

If she displeased him, he would not hesitate to show his dissatisfaction through anger.

Was that what they called love?

If that was supposed to be love, she wanted no part of it.

Now that Joyce was married, what was there to be afraid of?

Katrina let out an insulting laugh, not bothering to glance at Caiden again.

Caiden lowered his head, elbows resting on his knees, and ran his hand through his hair, overwhelmed by frustration.

How had he been so blind to see Katrina for the heartless woman she truly was?

He had even cut ties with Daniela because of her.

Alone in the quiet, empty living room, Caiden cried with regret in his heart.

While Caiden was absorbed by his regrets, Daniela's robotics company had made another remarkable breakthrough in the industry, achieving a worth of over a hundred billion dollars.

The company threw a grand celebration banquet.

As Joyce entered the venue, she saw Daniela on stage, gracefully holding her dress while delivering a speech. Cedric was by her side, holding a glass of water, his eyes sparkling with pride as he watched her.

Joyce's eyes stayed on Cedric for an extended moment before she turned her gaze to Alexander.

Alexander's expression was calm, yet Joyce sensed he must be feeling regret deep inside.

Daniela looked radiant and extremely competent, her successes now out of Alexander's league.

Daniela had lost all interest in Alexander.

Reflecting on this, Joyce let out a soft laugh. She had once felt superior to Daniela because she stole Alexander from Daniela.

Yet Daniela had moved on to Cedric, who clearly surpassed Alexander in every way.

It appeared she was the one whó had truly lost.

With a calm expression, Joyce brought her glass to her lips.

Following Daniela's speech, the crowd burst into applause. Joyce's eyes returned to Cedric, his face bright with happiness and pride.

It might have been her imagination, but Joyce felt that tonight, Daniela seemed closer to Cedric than ever.

As Daniela stepped down from the stage, she even extended her hand to Cedric. This was different from her usual self. Daniela had always been as independent as a lone wolf, always detached and unapproachable.

Joyce glanced at the person next to her, a smile tinged with sarcasm on her face. "Don't bother watching. I bet they'll end up together soon, if they aren't already."

Alexander remained silent, finishing his wine in a single, heavy swallow.
