Chapter 695

Kohen rushed back to Oiscoll and began searching for the person who matched the handwriting.

But three days had come and gone.

The handwriting he once knew so well had vanished without a trace.

Daniela broke into McCoy Group's system and ran comparisons across every handwriting sample available, but nothing came up.

There wasn't a single lead to follow.

Kohen returned, drained and breathless.

He told Daniela, "Someone's trying to block my investigation. I searched through the electronic signatures, even checked the archives, but it's like everything's been wiped. Some files are missing. Someone is making sure I don't find the person who wrote that note. There's something deeper going on here."

Daniela had already figured that out during her system breach. She didn't react.

Cedric stood beside her and spoke evenly. "lf nothing turns up, I'll join McCoy Group myself.Anything that once existed leaves a trail."

Kohen was mad. "What are you saying? Are you planning to reconnect with the McCoy family just because of this mysterious handwriting? And you want to join McCoy Group?"

Cedric didn't flinch. "Why not? Didn't you say Hamilton feels bad for everything that happened to me? He should be willing to make up for it, right?"

Kohen froze. He didn't know if Hamilton would agree.What he did know was that his father had missed Cedric's mother all these years. Every conflict between his parents had started because of Cedric's mother. Given his father's love for Cedric's mother, Kohen was sure his father would show favoritism towards Cedric.

Kohen pressed his lips together and stared at Cedric,then at Daniela. Their faces gave nothing away.

His thoughts drifted to something Nikolas once said -there was more than one way to get rid of someone.

Accidents.

lllness.

Whispers behind closed doors.

And the ache that came with losing a loved one.

If he explored every option, he'd find a solution.

The panic on Kohen's face slowly faded, replaced by something colder.

"Cedric, you should think twice before going down this road. You can't join McCoy Group like it's yours for the taking. That fortune isn't something you can claim just because you want to. Frankly, I don't believe this hadwriting is real. You probably forged it. You're using it as a way into McCoy Group, all so you can compete for the inheritance. Look, if you want some compensation, we'll give you. But if you're aiming for a share of the estate, forget it.That's never going to happen."

Cedric stayed composed. "Whether it's doable or not doesn't really depend on you."

Kohen couldn't even fake a smile anymore.He stared at Cedric and said coldly, "So you're ready to go against us now? All over a note we haven't even confirmed? Is that really worth it?"

To him, it was obvious-Cedric was doing this for Daniela.

Cedric didn't waste another word. His tone turned ice-cold. "Use every connection you have to track down that handwriting. Otherwise, leave."

Without waiting for a reply, he walked away. Kohen clenched his jaw and muttered, "Damn it."

Inside the villa, Cedric found Daniela sitting in front of her computer in the living room.

He knew she'd been at it for three days. Quietly, he stepped over and shut the laptop.

"There's no point. Everything on the inside has already been wiped clean. It's not something you can recover."

This had nothing to do with skil. That was just how the system had been built.

Daniela knew that. But she still wanted to give it a shot.

"I can dig further if I join McCoy Group.Handwriting is like muscle memory-it can be hidden, but never erased forever." Cedric tried to reassure her.

But she shook her head. "This is my responsibility.What I said to Kohen earlier was just to push him int o actually looking for the truth." She understood how dangerous inheritance battles could be in families like theirs. And she didn't think Cedric should risk himself just for money.
