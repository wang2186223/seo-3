Head bowed, Cedric held the towel Daniela had given him.

As time passed, everyone started to notice Daniela's growing distance from Cedric.

It wasn't just a physical distance but a breach in the silent understanding that once connected them.

Alexander was secretly pleased with this development.

He felt his time with the Harper family had been fruitful.

No matter how charming Cedric appeared, he had reached an impasse with Daniela.

The difference was that Daniela had once doggedly pursued Alexander for a decade, which gave him a sense of superiority.

Cedric's laugh was scornful. "Is that so? Well then, I hope you and Joyce find everlasting bliss."

Alexander couldn't even muster a smile.

From the corner of his eye, he saw Cedric's composed, assured demeanor and attempted to emulate it by stretching out his legs.

Cedric stood up.

Alexander, observing his profile, felt a mix of relief and smugness. "Cedric, just give up. You'll never win Daniela over. She revels in manipulating emotions. You can't outsmart her."
