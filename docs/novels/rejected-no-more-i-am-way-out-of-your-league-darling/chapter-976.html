The following day, Josh-once mocked for failing to lead McCoy Group-now stood proud as its head.

He sent invitations far and wide, filling the guest list with Oiscoll's most notable names. It became the event of the season.

His young bride, dressed in a flowing gown, glided from guest to guest with practiced ease.

Daniela stayed to the side while Carol leaned in and whispered, "Daniela, I just heard something wild."

Daniela glanced over. "What?"

"Felicia was the girl Mason fell hard for," said Carol."But she didn't feel the same. He never won her over."

Daniela raised an eyebrow and spotted Felicia across the crowd. The girl lifted her glass in a toast, her smile calm and sure. Daniela returned the gesture,amused.

"She's definitely not ordinary," said Daniela, watching her.

Midway through the ceremony, just as Daniela was about to step out, she heard someone calling after her. She turned and saw Felicia hurrying over, lifting her dress slightly so she could walk faster.

"I'm sorry | couldn't welcome you properly today, Ms.Harper," Felicia said, smiling.

Daniela lifted a brow, her expression unreadable. Felicia dropped the hem of her dress and met her gaze. Daniela's sharp features stirred a quiet envy in her. But it wasn't the kind that made her insecure. She knew better than to rely on beauty alone. Victoria had been beautiful too-and look how that ended. A woman needed more than a pretty face.

And that was exactly why Felicia wanted to move closer to Daniela.

She knew Daniela didn't just have beauty. She had brains too.

Felicia arrived in a fresh couture dress,all grace and polish. She offered a smile and spoke with care."Ms.Harper, thank you for meeting me. I know this was short notice, but I have a proposal I hope you'll consider."

Daniela didn't blink. She took another drink. "That won't happen."

Felicia gave a slow nod but couldn't stop herself from peeking toward Cedric again.

The man had a quiet pulL. He wasn't like Josh, who was already old. He wasn't like Mason either,emotional and impulsive. There was something about Cedric-something steady.

She pulled her attention back with a sigh. "If I had met him earlier, I might've taken a chance."
