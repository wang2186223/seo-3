She hadn't thought she'd run into Alexander there.

"You really went too far," Alexander said coldly.

Daniela arched an eyebrow at him.

"Oh really? She can give it a try," Daniela said, staring at the fading sunset with a neutral expression.

By the time she reached the foot of the mountain,she saw Cedric standing there, holding an umbrella.

The chilly, intense vibe around her melted away instantly as she saw him. "You're here!"

Cedric didn't say a word, just pressed his lips together,and tilted the umbrella towards her.

He had driven himself here. Daniela climbed into Cedric's car and her driver followed behind.

"Are you upset?" Daniela asked, though comforting others wasn't exactly her thing.

She'd never been one to hold back with those around her,always speaking her mind.

Cedric was an exception, though.

"If you're not going to tell me the truth, then don't bother trying to coax me. I'm not angry, and I don't need your pity."

Daniela's first try at offering comfort was a complete flop.

Later when she told Lillian about Cedric's response,Lillian burst out laughing so hard she had to hold her stomach. "Looks like you've offended him this time."

Daniela sighed. After Cedric dropped her off, he headed back to Elite Lux's lounge to rest.

Since they'd gotten together, they hadn't spent much time apart.

Honestly, Daniela wasn't used to being apart from him.

But for the moment, she wasn't sure how to comfort him-or maybe, she just hadn't figured out how to open up yet.

She wished Cedric could wait just a little longer.

In six months, she promised herself, she would have everything sorted out. By then, the danger would be behind her, and she would finally be able to tell the world that Cedric was her lawful husband.

And then, he would understand the depth of her feelings.
