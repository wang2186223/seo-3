How could I be hungry?!

"Don't worry. I swore I wouldn't eat You unless you want me to." He grinned, taking my hand and kissing it.

"The ice cream machine is broken. Do you want the Big Darrel to be a combo?' She asked.

It was silent a moment.

"$15.57 Pull around." She said as I could hear beeps in the background of her headset.

We did as we were told, and she was absolutely swamped. There were more people inside, and many were parked in a vacant lot to the side. Since it was off the interstate, I could only assume that this was a makeshift Truckstop. There were a lot of large 18-wheelers to the left, and to the right was farmland. "I don't think it's a good idea. You've made national headlines. We've Both Seen it. It would give your people Less closure. You can never go back to Your life as much as I can't either."

"I know but-" I was cut off again.

"Imagine your dead loved one, one that you're planning to bury knocks on your door to say 'hi', and can never return only they aren't a spirit or anything. They're alive. They're alive, with a 7'5 foot tall horned man with red eyes. How much closure would they have? They would look for you the rest of their lives, and could be imprisoned if they make too much noise." He warned.

"Whatever peace they'd get would be gone but what do you mean by that? Are they in danger?" I asked.

"Humans have their own police force. We have our own too. The Secret Police is a unit of humans and supernatural creature alike. They're Sole Purpose is to maintain the treaty of 1605 and 1607. That humans remain ignorant, and supernaturals keep to their own. That if either side breaks the law, then that individual belongs to the other." He said getting back on the interstate.

"But I didn't Do anything All we did was camp out there." I said a little defensively.

He sighed. "By crossing our border and knowing who we were, you all forfeited your freedom Willow. The addendum made years ago might have saved you if we had mind wiping potions or magic but they didn't follow the law. They just started killing because their leaders were away." He sounded frustrated.

"Then maybe you could appeal all that; you're probably still a prince until trial"

"I was a lazy Beta, and an even Worse Alpha substitute if they couldn't at Least follow orders a few days. My brother may be standing King but he has already ruled that I am to be captured and returned to the kingdom alive. He means to punish me."

I looked out the window, as it was silent a moment. "Then I have nowhere to go, and you vowed to keep me safe for now let's just drive and see where this takes us." I mumbled.

"I can do that." He said with a nod. "And now that you've eaten, I'll make sure you get your leg mended. It doesn't hurt, does it?" He asked out of concern, and I nodded.

"It's kind of numb, but the pain is there." I admitted.

"I'll make sure I get you something at the Meat Market, but when I take you, you Cannot leave my side." He warned.

" I don't think I want to go to a place where it's pretty obvious people are on the menu." I said quietly, and he chuckled.

"Humans are on nearly every menu. It is why the treaty keeps us secret." He scoffed. "I mean no ill towards your people, but every state has at least two. Humans in reality are food Willow. Supernaturals manage this world. Most of you live in blissful ignorance, especially since most meat now is farm raised." He said casually.

"We aren't beef cows!" I yelled a little angrier than I meant to, and he glanced at me briefly.

"It is a hard pill to swallow, but I gain Nothing by lying to you. Every supernatural, every Single One should be treated as a man eater. Unless they're a unicorn or herbivore, they aren't exactly vegan. I kind of wish I was lying, I really do." He said nothing else.

His gentle hand on my shoulder calmed me That was the mate bond; it gave me comfort, like I knew of all the stuff out there, he would shield me from it
